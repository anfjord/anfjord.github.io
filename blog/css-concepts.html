Padding on..

This week we learned how to style our websites with CSS.
One important concept to understand is how you use padding, margin and border.
Lets imagine that all HTML elements are wrapped in a box.
The box consists of: margins, borders, padding areas and the content (text, image etc)
The padding area of an element is the horizontal and vertical space that’s set around the actual content.
The border area extends the padding area to the area containing the borders of the element.
Margin is the space outside the border of that element.
If elements have no margins around them, they will bump right up against each other.
A good analogy that I found, was to think of padding like the space between your dining room table and wall (the border) and margin like your yard which is between you and your neighbor’s house.
